1. Introduction

Background Story

The world as we knew it is gone. A cascade of systemic failures — infrastructure collapse, resource depletion, and a global communications blackout — fractured civilization into isolated pockets of survivors. What remains is the wasteland: reclaimed by nature, contested by those with the will to endure.

In SectorZero, you are a survivor navigating this shattered landscape. The ground beneath your feet is unclaimed, the ruins around you are full of salvage, and every corner of the real world holds secrets waiting to be discovered. Walk. Explore. Claim your sector. Build what you need to outlast what's coming.

The world will not be reclaimed by waiting — it will be reclaimed one step at a time.

2. Getting Started

Main Interface

Icon Name Function
🗺️ Map View and explore the real-world map, track your GPS path, and spot nearby POIs.
🏴 Territory Claim, manage, and develop your owned sectors.
🎒 Resources View your inventory of salvaged materials, food, water, and equipment.

First Exploration Steps

  1. Open the app and allow location permissions when prompted. Set to While Using the App with Precise Location enabled.
  2. Navigate to the Map tab. You'll see your current position on the real-world map overlaid with the SectorZero grid.
  3. Tap Start Exploring and begin walking. Your GPS path will be drawn in real time.
  4. Look for glowing POI markers nearby. Approach them and tap to initiate a salvage scan.
  5. Once you've traced a closed loop (minimum 10 path points), return to the Territory tab to claim your first sector.

3. Map & Exploration

How to Explore

Exploration in SectorZero uses your device's GPS to track real-world movement. Open the Map tab and tap Start Exploring to begin recording your path. Walk around your area — the app draws a live breadcrumb trail. Your path data is used both for territory claiming and for discovering POIs in your vicinity.

POI Types

Icon Type Common Resources
🏚️ Abandoned Structure Scrap metal, wood planks, basic tools
🌿 Natural Area Edible plants, fresh water, natural fiber
🏪 Supply Cache Canned food, medical supplies, batteries
⚙️ Industrial Site Machine parts, fuel cells, rare metals
📦 Hidden Stash Random rare items, aether shards

GPS Signal Status

Status Accuracy Recommendation
🟢 Excellent < 5 meters Ideal for exploration and territory claiming.
🟡 Good 5–15 meters Functional. Territory claims may have slightly rough edges.
🟠 Fair 15–40 meters Move to an open area. Avoid claiming territory in this state.
🔴 Poor > 40 meters GPS unreliable. Wait for signal improvement before exploring.

Aether Energy

Each POI salvage scan consumes Aether Energy. Free accounts start with 5 energy. Energy replenishes over time, or can be restored instantly with Aether Energy packs purchased from the store. Archon tier subscribers have unlimited scan energy.

  • Standard POI scan: 1 Aether Energy
  • Enhanced scan (rare POI): 2 Aether Energy
  • Deep salvage: 3 Aether Energy

4. Territory System

Territory Requirements

  • Minimum 10 GPS path points to form a valid boundary
  • Enclosed area must be at least 100 m²
  • Cannot overlap with territory already claimed by another survivor
  • Start and end point must be within 30 meters of each other to close the loop

How to Claim

  1. Walk a closed loop outdoors while the Map tab is recording your path.
  2. Return close to your starting point until the path auto-closes (green indicator).
  3. Tap Claim Territory on the map overlay.
  4. Name your sector and confirm. The territory is now yours.
  5. Visit the Territory tab to view your sector details and begin construction.

Territory Management

  • Rename your sector from the Territory details screen
  • Abandon a territory to release it back to unclaimed land
  • Expand by claiming adjacent sectors and merging boundaries
  • Defend your territory by maintaining active structures and upgrades
  • Territories with no active maintenance may decay over time at higher game stages

9. Advanced Tips

🗺️ Efficient Territory Claiming

  • Plan your walk route before starting. Aim for compact, roughly rectangular loops to maximize area with fewer steps.
  • Avoid narrow corridors — territory with a width under ~10 meters may have reduced build slots.
  • Claim near POI clusters so you can regularly scan without leaving your territory.
  • Let GPS stabilize for 30–60 seconds before starting a claim walk, especially in dense urban areas.

🎒 Resource Management

  • Prioritize Water and Food stockpiles before expanding — these are your survival floor.
  • Stack POI scans on a single exploration route to maximize Aether Energy efficiency.
  • Check your inventory before building — don't start a structure you can't finish, as partial builds tie up materials.
  • Trade surplus resources in the marketplace rather than letting them cap out in your inventory.

🏗️ Building Planning

  • Always build a Shelter first — it's the prerequisite for every other structure.
  • Queue the Water Collector early; the 2-hour timer starts the moment you confirm, so start it before you stop playing.
  • A Workshop pays for itself quickly by reducing all future build times by 20%.
  • Don't neglect the Comms Relay if you plan to collaborate — communication range is a multiplier for every social mechanic.

10. FAQ

Q: Does SectorZero track my location in the background?

  • No. SectorZero only accesses your location while the app is open and actively in use. Background location is never requested or used. You can verify this in your iOS privacy settings at any time.

Q: I walked a loop but my territory claim failed. What happened?

  • The most common reasons are: (1) GPS accuracy was too low during the walk — check that signal was 🟢 Excellent or 🟡 Good. (2) The start and end points were more than 30 meters apart when you tried to close. (3) Part of your loop overlaps with an existing claimed sector. Try a new route in a more open area.

Q: Can I play SectorZero indoors or without moving?

  • Some features (inventory, comms, building management) are accessible indoors. However, exploration, territory claiming, and POI scanning all require real-world movement and a strong GPS signal. The game is designed as an outdoor experience.

Q: What happens to my territory if I don't play for a while?

  • In the current version, territories are persistent and do not decay from inactivity. Future updates may introduce maintenance mechanics at higher game stages. You'll always receive advance notice before any changes that affect existing territories.
